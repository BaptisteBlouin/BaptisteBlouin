#### 📰 Tech Brief — latest digest (2026-07-19)

- A widely discussed essay by Nik Suresh, amplified by Mitchell Hashimoto, argues that large organizations are in "AI psychosis" — executives mandate AI-centric strategies without ever having used AI tools themselves, while engineers resort to performative AI busywork (e.g., rewriting entire Go repos in Zig via AI) to satisfy internal token-leaderboard metrics.
- A study by researchers from three French and Italian universities found that AI advice dramatically degraded human judgment: willingness to say "I don't know" collapsed from 44% to 3%, accuracy dropped from 27% to 9%, while confidence rose from 30% to 76%. The study deliberately used questions where AI models typically fail (visual details from films) and employed Step 3.5 Flash, a model usually wrong on these questions, ensuring any degradation couldn't be explained as sensible delegation to a reliable tool. Monetary incentives helped only marginally — willingness to admit ignorance rose to 8% and accuracy to 16%, both still well below no-AI baselines. Wharton researchers previously coined the term "cognitive surrender" to describe the same phenomenon of people accepting incorrect AI answers.
- Claude Code has quietly shipped a Rust port of Bun (v1.4.0 preview) in its binary since the June 17 v2.1.181 release; startup improved ~10% on Linux with no user-visible disruption, reinforcing "boring is good" for runtime infrastructure. Simon Willison independently verified the claim by inspecting his Claude Code installation, finding `Bun v1.4.0` and 563 Rust source filenames embedded in the binary, confirming the Rust port of Bun is running in production across millions of devices. The Rust version has since been released as Bun canary, installable via `bun upgrade --canary`.
- Moonshot AI temporarily suspended new Kimi subscriptions due to overwhelming demand for Kimi K3, with GPU capacity pushed near its limits over 48 hours. Existing subscribers are unaffected, and the company is adding capacity and will reopen subscriptions in batches. Going forward, Moonshot will split membership into two plans: Kimi Membership for web, app, and work use, and Kimi Code Membership for coding workflows, to better match compute allocation.
- New York City's mayor proposed requiring landlords and realtors to disclose AI-generated or AI-edited images in rental listings, part of a broader "Rental Ripoff Report" tackling deceptive practices — a notable early example of municipal AI-disclosure regulation tied to consumer protection.
